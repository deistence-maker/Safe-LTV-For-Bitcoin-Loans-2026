## Introduction

If there is one concept you must understand before borrowing against Bitcoin, it is Loan-to-Value (LTV).

LTV determines:

- How much you can borrow  
- How risky your loan is  
- Whether you keep or lose your Bitcoin  

Most beginners lose their BTC not because loans are bad, but because they misunderstand LTV.

This guide explains everything in clear and simple terms.

---

## What Is LTV (Loan-to-Value)?

LTV measures how much you borrow compared to your Bitcoin’s value.

### Formula

LTV = Loan Amount ÷ Collateral Value

---

### Example

- Bitcoin value: $50,000  
- Loan amount: $25,000  

LTV = 50%

---

## Why LTV Is Important

LTV directly controls your liquidation risk.

- Higher LTV increases risk  
- Lower LTV reduces risk  

Lower LTV provides a stronger safety buffer against market volatility.

---

## LTV Risk Ranges

### 20–30% LTV

- Low risk  
- Strong safety buffer  
- Suitable for beginners  

---

### 30–40% LTV

- Moderate risk  
- Requires monitoring  

---

### 50% and above

- High risk  
- Increased likelihood of liquidation  

---

### 60–70% LTV

- Very high risk  
- Small price movements can trigger liquidation  

---

## What Happens When Bitcoin Price Drops

Consider this example:

- BTC value: $100,000  
- Loan: $50,000  
- LTV: 50%  

If BTC drops to $70,000:

New LTV = $50,000 ÷ $70,000 = approximately 71%

Your risk increases significantly. Further price drops may trigger liquidation.

---

## What Is Liquidation

Liquidation occurs when your LTV exceeds the platform’s allowed threshold.

The platform sells part or all of your Bitcoin to repay the loan.
